# Sponsor Diligence Sources

Free / public sources to consult before drafting the CIO memo. Use them in this order.

## 1. SEC EDGAR — Form D / D-A (always start here)

- Search the issuer's legal name in EDGAR full-text search: `https://efts.sec.gov/LATEST/search-index?q=%22{ISSUER+NAME}%22&forms=D,D/A`
- Or, if you have the EdgarTools MCP, call `edgar_text_search` with the legal name in quotes.
- For each Form D / D-A, capture: filing date, total offering size, amount sold, number of investors, exemption claimed (06b vs. 06c — the latter is general solicitation), minimum investment, related persons (sponsor principals).
- **Key reconciliation:** the marketed raise in the PPM versus the most recent D/A "Amount Sold." A 10× gap or larger is a flag worth surfacing.

## 2. SEC IAPD / Form ADV

- `https://adviserinfo.sec.gov/` — search the Manager entity. If the Manager claims to be unregistered, confirm. If registered, pull the latest Form ADV Part 2A (the "brochure") and reconcile its disclosures to the PPM. ADV brochures often disclose fees and conflicts more clearly than the PPM does.

## 3. FINRA BrokerCheck

- `https://brokercheck.finra.org/` — search the principal's name. Disclosures, customer disputes, and regulatory events appear here for any broker-dealer history.

## 4. State securities regulators

- For sponsors operating from a single state (Wyoming, Delaware, Arizona, etc.) — the state's securities division often publishes administrative actions. Worth a 30-second search.

## 5. Court records

- Federal: PACER (paid; useful for big-dollar matters).
- State: free state-level docket searches in the sponsor's HQ state and in any state where the fund holds property.

## 6. BBB and consumer-grade signals

- BBB profiles can flag pattern-of-complaint issues for sponsor-affiliated education businesses (which are common in retail-targeted private funds).

## 7. The sponsor's own website and adjacent businesses

- Look at the sponsor's marketing site, podcast appearances, paid coaching products, and any "Fund II" / "Fund III" announcements. The PPM may say "the Manager may sponsor other funds" — adjacent vehicles often already exist and create deal-allocation conflicts that the PPM treats as hypothetical.
- LinkedIn for the named principals — note prior employers and length of track record claimed.

## 8. Property records

- For real-estate funds: county property-tax records and state corporate registries can confirm whether the sponsor actually owns the assets it claims. Pick 2–3 named projects and verify ownership by the sponsor's stated entities.

## What to capture for the memo

Build a one-page sponsor-diligence appendix with:

- Sponsor's principal entities and CIKs
- Current Form D status (target raise vs. actual; investor count; first-sale date)
- Adviser registration status and (if registered) latest ADV
- Any disclosed disciplinary history
- List of parallel / successor funds known to exist
- Relevant adjacent businesses (education, brokerage, brokerage-adjacent, podcast)
- Sources cited as markdown links so the CIO can verify

Cite all of this in the memo's "Appendix — Sources Consulted." The CIO will check.
