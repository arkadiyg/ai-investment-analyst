### Volume Analysis

Volume = number of shares traded. Rising volume on up-days = "accumulation" (institutions buying); rising volume on down-days = "distribution" (institutions selling).

- **Recent pattern:** Price has crept higher recently while *volume fell* on the latest up-day (per stockinvest.us). This is a mild negative — rallies on fading volume are less convincing than rallies on expanding volume.
- **No clear distribution signal:** No evidence of heavy down-day volume that would suggest active selling.

**Important ETF caveat:** Volume signals on ETFs are *much weaker* than on individual stocks. Authorized participants (specialized market makers) create and redeem ETF shares directly with the fund, so on-exchange volume doesn't reliably reflect investor demand the way it does for, say, a single-stock equity. For DBMF specifically — a managed futures ETF whose underlying P&L is driven by the futures positions it holds (S&P, bonds, currencies, commodities) — volume is *not* a high-quality signal. Treat volume as a tiebreaker at best, not a primary driver.
