**About the security:** FTLS is the First Trust Long/Short Equity ETF. It is an actively managed exchange-traded fund (ETF) that holds long positions (bets that go up when prices rise) and short positions (bets that profit when prices fall) in U.S.-listed equities. Net assets ~$2.14B–$2.24B; expense ratio ~1.38%. It can be analyzed technically just like a stock: price, moving averages, momentum, volume, chart patterns.

### Trend Assessment
The primary trend is **up**. FTLS is trading near a fresh 52-week high ($72.92), having climbed from a 52-week low near $60.80 (a ~20% advance) over the trailing 12 months. The price sits **above both its 50-day and 200-day moving averages** (the 60-day SMA was ~$71.2 and the 200-day SMA was ~$68.6 as of mid-February 2026, and price has continued higher since).

- **Moving Average (MA):** the average closing price over a number of days. The 50-day MA reflects intermediate-term trend; the 200-day MA reflects long-term trend.
- **Golden cross alignment:** when the shorter MA (50-day) sits above the longer MA (200-day) and price is above both, it is a textbook bullish posture. FTLS shows this alignment.
- A NYSE article confirms FTLS reached a new 12-month high on April 11, 2026 — a hallmark of an established uptrend.

### Entry/Exit Zones
**For a momentum / trend-following entry (bullish thesis):**
- **Aggressive entry:** breakout buy on a daily close above **$73.15** (above Barchart R1/R2) with volume above the 20-day average.

- **Initial stop-loss:** a daily close below **$70.70** (recent micro-support) for tactical traders, or below **$68.60** (200-day MA) for position traders. A close below the 200-day would invalidate the bullish trend thesis.
- **Profit targets:**

  - Target 2: **$76.00–$77.00** (round-number / channel extension if breakout extends through Q2 2026).
- **Risk/reward at $72.88:** entry $72.88, stop $70.70, target 1 $74.50 → ~0.74:1 (poor); waiting for a $71.50 pullback entry with the same stop and target gives ~1.4:1 (acceptable).

**Bottom line for an inexperienced investor:** The chart is constructively bullish — price above both major moving averages, near 52-week highs, MACD positive. But buying *exactly at* the all-time high gives you almost no margin of safety. Either (a) wait for a pullback toward $71–$72 and the 50-day MA, or (b) wait for a confirmed breakout close above $73.15 on heavier volume. Use a stop-loss; do not "hope" through a break of the 200-day MA at $68.60.
