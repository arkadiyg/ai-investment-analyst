## Fundamentals Analyst Signal Report — AEIS

**Signal:** bullish
**Confidence:** 72

*Date: 2026-05-24 | Price: $324.47 (close 2026-05-22) | Market Cap: ~$12.35B | EV: ~$12.79B*

### Profitability Metrics

(Profitability metrics measure how efficiently a company converts revenue into profit and capital into returns.)

- **Gross margin** (revenue minus direct cost of producing goods, as a % of revenue): **TTM 37.9%**; Q1-2026 jumped to **39.3%** (+212 bps YoY). FY2025 37.7% vs FY2024 35.7% and FY2023 35.8% — clear upward trajectory as the semicap cycle and data-center power product mix improve.
- **Operating margin** (profit after operating costs, before interest/tax): **TTM 10.8%**; Q1-2026 reached **13.4%** (+580 bps YoY). FY2025 10.3% recovered sharply from FY2024's depressed 2.5–5.1% trough.
- **Net margin**: **TTM 8.9%**; Q1-2026 **13.1%** (+697 bps YoY) — strong operating leverage as revenue scales over a relatively fixed R&D/SG&A base.
- **ROE** (return on equity — net income / shareholder equity): **TTM 11.8%**, FY2025 11.5%, up from FY2024's 4.6%. Healthy but not exceptional.
- **ROIC** (return on invested capital — operating profit after tax / total capital): **TTM 8.6%**, FY2025 13.0%. ROIC > likely cost of capital (~8–9%) — value-creating.
- Versus peers (TTM): AEIS gross margin 37.9% trails MKSI 46.9% / ENTG 45.2% but beats COHR 36.7%, UCTT 15.9%, ICHR 10.1%. AEIS op margin 10.8% beats all listed peers except ENTG (12.1%).

### Growth Metrics

- **Revenue**: Q1-2026 $511M, **+26.3% YoY** — major reacceleration. FY2025 $1,798.8M, +21.4% YoY, recovering from FY2024's $1,482M trough (-10.5% YoY). Sequential quarters: $404.6M → $441.5M → $463.3M → $511M.
- **EPS** (earnings per share — net income per diluted share): Q1-2026 **$1.58 diluted, +143% YoY**; FY2025 diluted EPS $3.84 vs FY2024 $1.43 (+169%). TTM growth muted (~0.4%) only because trailing window still includes depressed Q1-2025 base — forward momentum far stronger.
- **FCF** (free cash flow — operating cash flow minus capital expenditure): FY2025 FCF $41M (rebound from FY2024's -$2.6M); FY2023 $67M. Q1-2026 FCF -$42.6M reflects working-capital build + capex up 163% YoY for capacity expansion (seasonal/growth investment, not deterioration).
- **EPS estimate miss**: Q1-2026 EPS missed consensus by 3.7% despite 0.75% revenue beat — diluted share count expanded to 42.2M (vs 38.6M FY2025) due to convertible-note dilution at higher stock price.

### Balance Sheet Health

(Balance sheet shows what the company owns vs owes at a point in time.)

- **Cash & equivalents**: $699.5M (Q1-2026). Total debt: $1,136M. **Net debt ~$437M** — manageable at ~1.5x TTM EBITDA.
- **Current ratio** (current assets / current liabilities; >1 is healthy): **1.59 TTM**. FY2025 current ratio dropped from 4.4 to 1.59 because convertible notes ($567.5M) reclassified to current — technical, not operational.
- **Quick ratio** (excludes inventory): 1.13 — adequate.
- **Debt/equity**: 0.82 — moderate; down from FY2023's 1.24.
- **Interest coverage** (EBIT / interest expense; >5x healthy): **TTM 11.6x**, Q1-2026 ~17x — very comfortable.
- Goodwill & intangibles $418.5M (~16% of assets) — reasonable.

### Cash Flow Quality

(High-quality earnings convert to cash; persistent gaps signal accounting risk.)

- FY2025 OCF $233.3M vs net income $148.4M — **OCF/NI = 1.57x** — high-quality earnings.
- FY2024 OCF/NI = 2.4x (working-capital release).
- FY2023 OCF/NI = 1.63x.
- **Capex intensity**: FY2025 capex $107.4M = ~6% of revenue (elevated for Malaysia facility ramp); Q1-2026 annualized ~$146M = 7%+ — heavy investment cycle.
- **SBC** (stock-based compensation): FY2025 $55.7M ≈ 3% of revenue.
- FCF conversion FY2025 ~28% (weighed by capex); normalized conversion ~65–70%.

### Earnings Quality Notes

- **Convertible-note dilution risk**: diluted share count jumped to 42.2M Q1-2026 vs 37.8M FY2024 (+12%) — converts in-the-money, dampening EPS leverage. Worth modeling fully diluted scenarios.
- Q1-2026 negative FCF (-$42.6M) and -$6M OCF deserve monitoring — if persists 2+ quarters, becomes a yellow flag.
- FY2024 included $40.9M non-operating income and a tax benefit (-$3.9M tax expense) that flattered headline net income — FY2025/Q1-2026 recovery is real operating recovery, not accounting noise.
- R&D held at 11–13% of revenue through the downturn — disciplined, not earnings-managed cuts. Positive franchise signal.
- No revenue-recognition red flags; receivables turnover stable ~5–6x; goodwill stable with no impairments.

**Bottom line:** AEIS is firmly in cyclical recovery — margins, revenue and earnings reaccelerating with clean operating leverage. Balance sheet healthy, earnings quality sound. Principal caveat: valuation. Forward ~30x P/E / ~25x EV/EBITDA leaves little margin for cyclical disappointment, and convertible dilution is a structural drag. **Bullish at 72 confidence** — operating trajectory too strong to ignore, valuation tempers conviction.
