## Growth Analyst Signal Report — AEIS

**Signal:** bullish
**Confidence:** 78

### TAM Analysis
(Total addressable market = total annual dollars potentially spendable on a company's products)

AEIS plays into three overlapping mega-cycles, all inflecting in 2026:

- **Semiconductor Wafer Fab Equipment (WFE):** SEMI now forecasts WFE at ~$135B in 2026 and growing to ~$156B in 2027 (Morgan Stanley revised 2026 WFE growth from +5% to +10%, ~$128B). AEIS supplies the RF and DC power-delivery sub-systems (plasma generators, remote plasma sources, low-frequency match networks) embedded in deposition and etch tools from Applied Materials, Lam Research, and ASML. Power is roughly 5-8% of a leading-edge tool's bill of materials, implying a serviceable market of ~$7-10B that is growing with advanced logic, HBM (high-bandwidth memory used in AI chips), and gate-all-around (next-gen transistor) buildouts. AEIS FY2025 semi-equipment revenue was $839.9M — meaningful share but ample headroom.
- **Data Center Computing:** This is the disruptive growth vector. AEIS-supplied 800V/1000V high-voltage DC rack power architectures let hyperscalers (large cloud operators like Microsoft, Google, Meta) pack more GPUs per rack. AEIS FY2025 data center revenue was $587.3M, up 107% YoY from $284M in FY2024 — and Q1 2026 saw the segment more than double again YoY.
- **Industrial & Medical:** A ~$3-5B niche where AEIS plays in plasma cutting, semiconductor metrology, and medical imaging power. Slower-growth ballast but high-margin.

Combined TAM is conservatively $30-45B today versus AEIS TTM revenue of ~$1.82B — penetration is in the low-single-digit percentages.

- Q1 2026 revenue of $511M beat prior guidance; management **raised FY2026 growth target from "high-teens" to "low-to-mid-20s%"** — second straight year of 20%+ growth.
- Gross margin hit **40.1% in Q1 2026** (highest since 2019 Artesyn deal), with another 20-50 bps of expansion guided for Q2.
- Data center operating margin expanded 560 bps YoY to 19.1% — proves the segment is not "buying revenue."
- Q2 2026 guide: ~$540M (+5.7% QoQ) with semi + I&M re-acceleration on top of sustained data-center momentum, implying a ~$2.1-2.2B annualized run-rate.

Operating leverage is the story: revenue up ~37% from the 2024 trough, but operating income up ~360% (from $36.6M to a Q1-annualized ~$273M). R&D and SG&A are largely fixed.

### Competitive Positioning
(Switching costs = pain/cost a customer incurs to change vendors)

- **Designed-in moat:** Power supplies for semicap tools are qualified into specific etch/deposition chambers over 18-36 month cycles. Once spec'd, swap-outs are rare because re-qualification risks wafer yield. AEIS shares this oligopoly position with MKS Instruments (MKSI) and Comet.
- **Hyperscaler differentiation:** The 800V HVDC rack design — accelerated by the June 2024 Airity Technologies acquisition (GaN-based, gallium-nitride high-frequency power conversion) — is category-creating. Legacy 48V rack power cannot scale to NVIDIA Blackwell/Rubin-class racks pulling 130-250 kW. AEIS is reportedly an early-mover with at least one Tier-1 hyperscaler and tied to NVIDIA reference designs.
- **Peer benchmark:** AEIS trades at ~6.8x P/S vs. MKSI ~2-3x and ENTG ~4-5x. The premium is earned on growth (AEIS +26% YoY vs. MKSI ~+10%) and on data-center mix (MKSI has effectively zero hyperscaler power exposure). UCTT and ICHR are more cyclical pure-play subsystem names with no comparable data-center optionality.
- **Disruption angle:** This is one of the few "old industrial" names with a real AI-infrastructure picks-and-shovels story, similar to Vertiv (VRT)'s re-rating arc.

### Key Catalysts
- **800V/HVDC ramp:** First production-volume hyperscaler deployments expected H2 2026, with sequential step-ups through 2027 as NVIDIA Rubin / Vera Rubin Ultra reference designs proliferate.
- **WFE up-cycle:** SEMI's $135B 2026 / $156B 2027 forecast plus accelerating HBM4 capex should lift the semi-equipment segment back toward $1B+ annualized as Lam and Applied refresh tools for gate-all-around / 2nm.
- **Margin expansion runway:** Long-term target of 40-43% GM and ~25% operating margin (vs. ~13% today) — if hit, EPS could compound to $7-9 over 2-3 years.
- **M&A optionality:** Strong balance sheet (cash > debt, current ratio 1.59) and proven integration playbook (Artesyn, Airity) leave room for additional power-electronics tuck-ins (SiC/GaN players, BBU/UPS adjacencies).
- **Industrial & Medical recovery:** Segment fell to $282M in FY2025 from $474M in FY2023; a cyclical rebound alone is worth $150-200M of high-margin revenue.

### Risks to Growth Thesis
- **Valuation:** Stock trades at ~84x trailing P/E and ~7x sales — priced for 25%+ growth and continued margin expansion. Any guide-down would compress the multiple sharply (the stock has roughly tripled from late-2024 lows).
- **Hyperscaler concentration:** Data-center growth depends on a small number of customers (likely Microsoft, Meta, Google, plus NVIDIA reference platforms). Loss of a single design-in, or a hyperscaler in-sourcing power, would be material.
- **WFE cyclicality:** Semicap is famously cyclical. China export controls, a memory-pricing crash, or a foundry capex pause could quickly turn the semi-equipment segment negative.
- **Competitive response:** MKSI, Delta Electronics (Taiwan), Vicor, and Infineon are all chasing HVDC rack power. AEIS's first-mover advantage may be 12-18 months, not 3-5 years.
- **Margin durability:** 40% GM has historically been the ceiling for AEIS post-Artesyn; the 43% long-term target requires data-center mix to hold while semi-equipment volumes recover.
- **AI capex digestion:** A genuine hyperscaler capex pause in 2H 2026 would hit AEIS's fastest-growing, highest-multiple segment hardest.
