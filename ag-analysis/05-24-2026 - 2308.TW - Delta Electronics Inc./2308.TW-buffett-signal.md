## Buffett Analyst Signal Report — 2308.TW (Delta Electronics, Inc.)

**Signal:** neutral (leaning bearish on valuation, bullish on quality)
**Confidence:** 65

> **Ticker note:** Originally briefed as "2371.TW" but on TWSE **2371 = Tatung Co.**, not Delta. Delta Electronics trades as **2308.TW**. This report analyzes the company that was named — Delta Electronics, 2308.TW.

### Plain-English Primer (for an inexperienced investor)
- **Moat:** A durable competitive advantage that lets a company earn high returns for many years.
- **Owner earnings / Free cash flow (FCF):** Cash the company can distribute after maintaining the business. Buffett's preferred profit measure.
- **DCF (Discounted Cash Flow):** Estimating today's fair value by projecting future cash and discounting it back at a required rate of return.
- **ROE (Return on Equity):** Profit divided by equity. >15% sustained = high-quality business.
- **Margin of safety:** Discount between price paid and intrinsic value — Buffett wants ≥25–40%.

### Moat Assessment

Delta is one of the world's largest **switching power-supply** (SPS) makers, with an estimated ~50%+ global share in server/telecom power and a dominant position in AI-server power-shelf and busbar modules for NVIDIA reference designs (GB200/GB300).

1. **Scale / cost advantage (strong):** Largest power-electronics manufacturing footprint globally (Taiwan, China, Thailand, India, Mexico). Per-unit cost on a 5.5 kW PSU is structurally below Lite-On, Chicony, Bel Fuse.
2. **Switching costs (moderate, rising):** Hyperscaler and NVIDIA reference designs are co-engineered over 18–24 months. Re-qualifying a second source for a 12-kV DC busbar risks data-center uptime — customers stick.
3. **R&D-driven IP (strong):** ~8–9% of revenue on R&D (~NT$45B in 2025). Only Asian supplier shipping 97.5%-efficient titanium-class PSUs at scale; top-3 in two-phase liquid cooling.
4. **Brand / quality reputation (moderate):** Default "safe" pick for data-center engineers and EV OEMs (Tesla, BMW, Volvo, multiple China EV OEMs use Delta on-board chargers).
5. **Network effects:** Limited (B2B components).
6. **Pricing power (demonstrated):** Q1 2026 gross margin a **record 37%** despite copper inflation and stronger TWD — Delta is capturing AI-mix premium, not just passing through cost.

**Moat verdict:** Wide and **widening** in AI/data-center power; narrower in legacy industrial automation and EV chargers (where Inovance, Megmeet are intense). Net: a real but cyclically-exposed moat — "wide" in AI, "narrow" elsewhere.

### Margin of Safety

- **Current price (May 23, 2026):** ~NT$2,095
- **Base-case intrinsic value:** ~NT$1,750
- **Implied margin of safety:** **NEGATIVE ~20%** (price *above* base fair value)
- Even the bull case (NT$2,600) is only ~25% upside — below Buffett's 40%+ requirement.
- Street consensus target ~NT$2,697 effectively treats the bull case as the base case.
- Trailing P/E ~42–47x and forward ~22–25x are well above the 10-year median (~25x trailing). The AI premium is already priced in.

**Verdict: no margin of safety today.** A patient Buffett-style buyer waits for **NT$1,200–1,400** (a 30–45% drawdown — the kind seen in 2022–2023 capex digestion cycles).

### Management Quality

- **Founder culture:** Bruce C.H. Cheng founded Delta in 1971; remains Honorary Chairman. Cheng family still active — Ping Cheng (Chairman/CEO, ~2.1% stake); Chong Hua Cheng (exec, ~4.1%).
- **Largest holder:** Deico International (Cheng-family vehicle) ~10% — meaningful skin in the game, stable voting block.
- **Capital allocation:** Disciplined. ~8–9% of sales into R&D (top-quartile hardware); capex into high-IRR areas (AI power, liquid cooling, India fab). M&A (Eltek, Trihedral, LOYTEC, March Networks) strategic, not empire-building.
- **Dividends:** 20+ years of payments; NT$7.00/share most recent (~0.3% yield only because the stock has tripled). Payout ratio ~30–35% — reinvestment runway intact.
- **Buybacks:** Not a regular tool (typical for Taiwan compounders); modest treasury activity only.
- **Balance sheet:** Net cash (cash NT$110B > total debt). D/E ~19–31%, well under Buffett's 0.5 ceiling. Interest coverage a non-issue.
- **ROE 27%** (well above 15% hurdle); **operating margin ~17–19%** (above 15% hurdle, expanding with AI mix).
- **Governance:** Among the highest-rated Taiwan tech firms; transparent IR; no major related-party concerns.

**Management quality: high.** Founder-aligned, conservative balance sheet, capital reinvested at >20% incremental returns. Exactly the type of operator Buffett admires.

### Key Risks

1. **AI capex digestion / cycle peak.** Hyperscaler capex +60% YoY in Q1 2026; history shows 18–24-month digestion cycles. A pause would compress both growth and multiple — a 1–2σ re-rating could halve the share price.
2. **Margin mean-reversion.** 37% GM is a record; Lite-On, Vertiv and Chinese entrants are racing in. A 200–400 bps GM loss = ~15–20% EPS hit.
3. **Geopolitical / supply chain.** China manufacturing exposure; US-China decoupling and Taiwan tensions are tail risks for any TWSE name.
4. **EV slowdown.** EV powertrain/charging (~10–12% of revenue) exposed to softer Western EV demand.
5. **Currency.** Revenue largely USD; costs in TWD/CNY/THB. TWD strength is a 2025–2026 headwind.
6. **Valuation risk > operational risk.** At 25x+ forward, even good results can produce negative returns if the multiple compresses.
7. **Succession.** Cheng family still central; long-term succession less battle-tested than e.g. TSMC.

### Buffett Verdict

Delta is **the right kind of business** — wide moat, founder-aligned management, fortress balance sheet, 27% ROE, growing FCF, secular AI-power tailwind. It checks nearly every Buffett quality box. But Buffett's rule is "wonderful company at a fair price." At ~NT$2,095, the market is pricing the bull-case DCF as the base. There is no margin of safety. Action: **add to watchlist; buy aggressively only on a 30–40% drawdown to NT$1,200–1,400.** Today: **neutral/hold** for owners; **avoid initiating** new positions.
