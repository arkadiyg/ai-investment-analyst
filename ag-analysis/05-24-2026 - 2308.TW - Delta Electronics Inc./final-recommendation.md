#### Signal Agreement

**All five analysts agree on the business:** Delta is the dominant AI-server power supplier (>60% AI PSU market share), co-engineered NVIDIA's 800V HVDC reference design for GB300, posted record Q1 2026 financials (+36% revenue, +101% net income, 37% gross margin), and runs a fortress balance sheet (~NT$45–85B net cash, 27% ROE).

**They disagree sharply on price.** Growth and Sentiment see the secular AI tailwind as still under-priced; Buffett, Fundamentals, and Technical see the AI premium as fully embedded. The Growth–Fundamentals split on long-run gross margin (38–40% vs. 32–34% steady-state) is the single biggest valuation lever. The Technical–Sentiment split on the recent -12% pullback (rollover vs. buyable dip) is the near-term tactical question.

**Translation:** the risk here is not that the company breaks — it's that **the multiple compresses**. A valuation-driven drawdown of 30–40% is possible *even if* the business keeps growing.

#### Investment Thesis

Delta Electronics is one of the cleanest "picks-and-shovels" plays on AI infrastructure. AI servers need orders of magnitude more electrical power than older servers, and the industry is moving to a new high-voltage DC ("HVDC") power architecture to handle it. Delta co-designed that architecture with NVIDIA, ships the most units in the world, and now sells the power racks, liquid-cooling units, and battery backups as one integrated stack — so its dollar content per AI rack rises every generation. Q1 2026 confirmed this is real, not narrative: revenue +36% YoY, net income +101% YoY, and a record 37% gross margin.

The problem is **price**. The stock is up roughly 6x in twelve months and trades at ~54x forward 2026 earnings — 2–3x its peer group. Our Buffett-style intrinsic value work pegs base-case fair value at **NT$1,750** vs. a spot of **NT$2,095** — there is no margin of safety today. Three of our five analysts are not bullish at this level, and the chart has just rolled over: RSI 36.5, MACD negative, an 8-to-0 short-term sell-signal unanimity, and a -12% slide from the May 7 all-time high (NT$2,370) in two weeks.

**The verdict therefore tilts toward HOLD, with a small starter buy for new money.** We are willing to own a great business at a stretched price, but we will not pretend that a 10% full-size position is appropriate when the technical regime is in active rollover and the entry is above fair value. We size to survive: a **3.0–4.5% starter** with explicit add-on rungs at NT$1,850, NT$1,500–1,700, and NT$1,200–1,400. This lets us participate in the AI-power thesis while preserving dry powder for the kind of -30% to -40% drawdown that parabolic large-caps routinely deliver after running this far this fast.

**Signal weighting:** We are weighting Buffett, Fundamentals, and Risk Manager most heavily because the disagreement among analysts is entirely about valuation — and valuation discipline is the function those three serve. We discount Growth and Sentiment slightly because both explicitly acknowledge the trade is crowded and the AI premium is in the price; they are bullish in spite of valuation, not because of it. Technical confirms the near-term setup is unfavorable for chasing strength. If we had a fresh -30% pullback to NT$1,500, the weighting would flip — Growth and Sentiment would dominate and we would size much larger.

**For an inexperienced investor:** "HOLD" means if you already own it, don't sell on this dip — the business is fine. "STARTER 3–4%" means a new buyer should commit only a small slice of their portfolio at this price and explicitly plan to buy more if the price falls toward NT$1,500 or NT$1,200 with the business still on track. Do **not** allocate a full position at NT$2,095 — the math doesn't justify it, and three of our five analysts agree.

#### Key Risk Factors

1. **Multiple compression** is the biggest risk. At ~54x forward earnings (versus a peer average ~22x), even a *modest* re-rating to ~30–35x would mean -25% to -40% on the stock — even if Delta hits every earnings number.
2. **AI capex digestion.** Hyperscalers (Microsoft, Meta, Google, Amazon) grew capex +60% YoY in Q1 2026. Historically these cycles digest for 18–24 months after such prints. A single guide-down from any one of them is the most likely trigger for a sharp Delta drawdown.
3. **NVIDIA customer concentration.** Co-designing the 800V power architecture with NVIDIA is a *moat* — but it also makes Delta a single-point-of-failure to NVIDIA's roadmap. Any architecture shift or hyperscaler design-win loss to Lite-On or Vertiv would re-price the stock.
4. **Gross-margin mean reversion.** 37% gross margin is a record. As Lite-On, Acbel, Vertiv, and Chinese competitors add capacity, GM could normalize toward 32–34%. Each 100 bps of GM loss is roughly 5–7% off EPS.
5. **Taiwan / China geopolitics.** Low probability, very high impact. A permanent overhang on any TWSE name.
6. **TWD strength.** ~80% of revenue is non-TWD; stronger TWD compresses reported margins.
7. **Crowded positioning.** ~75–78% foreign ownership and 18 Buy / 1 Sell consensus mean the marginal buyer is scarce. A global risk-off episode could force selling regardless of fundamentals.
8. **AI-capex factor concentration in the portfolio.** Verify total AI-capex exposure (NVDA + TSMC + AVGO + VRT + ASML + Delta) is below **25–30%** before adding. Verify Taiwan-domicile exposure (TSMC + MediaTek + Hon Hai + Delta) is below **15–20%**.
