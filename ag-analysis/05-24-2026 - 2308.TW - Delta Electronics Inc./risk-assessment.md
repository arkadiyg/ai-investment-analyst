## Risk Manager Assessment — 2308.TW (Delta Electronics, Inc.)

*Prepared by Risk Management, AG Capital | Date: 2026-05-24 | Spot: NT$2,095*

> **Plain-English orientation for an inexperienced investor.** This memo answers three questions: (1) What are our five analysts saying in aggregate? (2) How risky is this stock in isolation? (3) How big a position can we responsibly take given the risk?
>
> **Key terms used below:**
> - **Signal:** Each analyst's bottom-line view (bullish = buy, bearish = sell, neutral = hold).
> - **Confidence:** Analyst's self-rated certainty (0–100).
> - **Volatility:** How wildly the price moves around its average. Higher = more risk per dollar invested.
> - **Position size:** The % of the portfolio we put in one stock.
> - **Correlation:** How much a stock moves in lockstep with another stock or theme. High correlation = if one falls, the other usually does too.
> - **Margin of safety:** Buying at a discount to fair value so you can still profit if you're wrong.

### Signal Agreement / Divergence

**Points of agreement (5/5 analysts):**
- Delta is a high-quality, wide-moat business with dominant share in AI-server power.
- Q1 2026 results (revenue +36% YoY, net income +101% YoY, record 37% gross margin) confirm a real operating inflection.
- Management is high quality, balance sheet is net cash (~NT$45–85B cash above all debt).
- The stock is expensive on every absolute valuation metric.

**Points of disagreement:**
- **Valuation vs. growth tradeoff.** Growth/Sentiment say the secular AI tailwind justifies paying up; Buffett/Fundamentals say even the bull case (NT$2,600) gives <25% upside, below a sensible margin of safety.
- **Near-term direction.** Technical and Buffett see a multi-week corrective phase ahead (entry zones NT$1,200–1,950); Sentiment sees the recent 12% pullback as a buyable post-earnings dip.
- **Margin sustainability.** Fundamentals flags 37% GM as "unprecedented, model 32–34% steady-state"; Growth models margins drifting to **38–40%**. This is a 400–800 bps spread on the most important earnings driver.

**Implication for risk:** When 5 analysts agree on the business but disagree sharply on price, the risk is **not** that the company breaks — it's that the **multiple compresses**. That is a "valuation-driven drawdown" scenario, which can deliver -30% to -40% even if the business keeps growing. This is exactly the risk profile we must size for.

### Volatility Classification

**Verdict: HIGH volatility.**

Reasoning (in plain English):
1. **The chart shape.** A 6x rally in 12 months (NT$365 → NT$2,370) followed by a -12% pullback in 2 weeks is textbook parabolic behavior. Stocks in this state routinely swing 5–10% on a single news headline.
2. **Distance from trend.** Price sits **+275% above the 200-day moving average** and **+127% above the 50-day**. Healthy uptrends typically run 5–20% above the 50-day. This much air below the stock means a "normal" mean-reversion move could be 30–40%.
3. **Beta to a single factor.** Delta is now a high-beta proxy for AI-capex sentiment. The four major US hyperscalers' capex prints drive the stock more than Delta's own earnings. Any of MSFT/META/GOOG/AMZN trimming AI guidance could trigger a 10–20% gap.
4. **Crowding.** ~75–78% foreign ownership and 18 Buy / 1 Sell consensus mean almost everyone who wants to be in is already in. Marginal buyers are scarce; if foreign flows reverse, supply hits an empty bid stack.
5. **Momentum confirms.** RSI 36.5, MACD negative, 8/0 sell-signal unanimity across momentum oscillators. The stock is in a volatility-expansion regime, not a quiet base.

Although Delta is a US$170B large cap (which would normally suggest medium volatility), the post-rally regime and factor exposure clearly put it in the **HIGH** bucket.

### Recommended Position Size

**Volatility-adjusted ceiling for HIGH volatility names: 10% of portfolio.**

**Recommended Delta position: 3.0% – 4.5% of portfolio (a "starter" or "third-size" position).**

Why well below the 10% ceiling:
- **Three of five analysts are not bullish at current price.** A full-size 10% allocation is reserved for high-conviction, multi-analyst bullish setups. This isn't one.
- **No margin of safety** (Buffett base IV ~NT$1,750 vs spot NT$2,095, ~ -20%). Sizing must shrink when entry price is above fair value.
- **Asymmetric drawdown risk.** A real correction toward the 50-day MA implies -55% downside; even a back-test of the late-April breakout zone is -7% to -12%. At 4% sizing, a -40% drawdown costs the portfolio 160 bps — recoverable. At 10%, it costs 400 bps — painful and slow to recover.
- **Adds room to scale.** Growth analyst's "starter position with room to add on AI-capex-fear pullback" thinking is the right operating posture. Reserve 3–5% of additional dry powder to deploy if the stock back-tests NT$1,850 (technical entry) or NT$1,500–1,700 (fundamentals entry).
- **If price reaches Buffett's NT$1,200–1,400 zone with the business still intact, the position can be scaled toward the 8–10% ceiling.**

### Portfolio Risk Impact

Impact of adding a 3.0%–4.5% Delta position to a diversified portfolio:

1. **Sector concentration (AI / semis / Taiwan tech).** Most AG Capital portfolios are likely already long the AI capex theme via names such as NVIDIA, TSMC, Broadcom, Vertiv, ASML, Arista, or hyperscaler equities. Delta has very high correlation to this basket (0.7–0.9 estimated on factor regression). Treat Delta's economic exposure as **additive to total AI-capex bucket**, not as diversification.
   - **House rule recommendation:** Total AI-capex bucket (Delta + TSMC + NVDA + VRT + Arista + similar) capped at **25–30% of portfolio**. Confirm headroom before adding Delta.
2. **Taiwan / geopolitical concentration.** Delta is TWSE-listed with Taiwan, China, and SE Asia manufacturing. If a Delta position is added on top of an existing TSMC, MediaTek, or Hon Hai position, **Taiwan-domicile exposure** rises in tandem. Cap total Taiwan-listed equity exposure at **15–20%** of portfolio.
3. **FX exposure.** Position is TWD-denominated; ~80% of Delta's revenue is non-TWD. Two-layered FX risk: TWD/USD on the share price, plus USD/TWD impact on Delta's reported margins. Hedge or accept; do not ignore.
4. **Correlation/factor exposure.** Delta now functions as a high-beta AI-capex proxy. In a 2022-style AI-sentiment unwind, expect Delta to fall **1.5–2x** the AI basket. Stress-test the portfolio for a 30% Delta drawdown coinciding with a 15% NVDA/TSMC drawdown.
5. **Risk-budget contribution.** A 4% position in a name with assumed 50% annualized volatility contributes roughly **200 bps of stand-alone vol** to the portfolio — manageable, but meaningful. Combined with correlated AI names, this can push the portfolio's AI-factor risk over comfortable limits quickly.

### Key Risk Factors

Ranked by combined probability × impact over the next 6–12 months:

1. **Valuation / multiple compression (HIGHEST).** At ~54x fwd 2026 EPS and ~30–35x fwd 2027 EPS — 2–3x peer multiples — even good earnings can produce negative stock returns if the AI premium fades. A re-rating to a still-rich 25x fwd 2027 implies -25% to -30% downside.
2. **AI capex digestion cycle.** Hyperscaler capex grew +60% YoY in Q1 2026. Historical capex cycles digest for 18–24 months after such prints. Any guide-down from MSFT/META/GOOG/AMZN is the single biggest catalyst for a sharp Delta drawdown.
3. **Customer concentration / NVIDIA ecosystem dependency.** Co-designed 800V architecture with NVIDIA is a moat *and* a single-point-of-failure. An NVIDIA roadmap shift or hyperscaler design-win loss to Lite-On or Vertiv could re-price the stock.
4. **Gross-margin mean-reversion.** Record 37% GM may normalize to 32–34% as Lite-On, Acbel, Vertiv, and Chinese competitors add capacity. Each 100 bps of GM loss ≈ ~5–7% EPS.
5. **Taiwan / China geopolitics.** Tail risk applicable to all TWSE names. Low base-rate probability, but very high impact if it materializes.
6. **TWD appreciation / FX.** Stronger TWD compresses reported margins (USD-denominated revenue translated into stronger TWD). Persistent FX headwind through 2026.
7. **Crowded positioning / factor de-risking.** ~75–78% foreign ownership and consensus Buy mean a global risk-off episode can force forced selling regardless of fundamentals.
8. **EV segment weakness.** ~10–12% of revenue exposed to softer Western EV demand and Chinese price war — drag, not thesis-breaker.
9. **Execution risk on 800V HVDC / SOFC.** Solid-state transformers and SOFC commercialization are genuinely hard; a 6–12 month slip would let Vertiv close the gap and disappoint the growth narrative.
10. **Succession risk.** Cheng family still central; long-dated, low-probability but worth tracking.

### Risk Flags

Flags the Portfolio Manager should weigh heavily before authorizing the trade:

- **FLAG 1 — No margin of safety.** Spot NT$2,095 sits **20% above** the Buffett base intrinsic value of NT$1,750. Even the bull-case IV (NT$2,600) offers only ~25% upside — below the 30–40% minimum we typically demand for HIGH-volatility names. **This is the single largest reason to keep sizing small.**
- **FLAG 2 — Three of five analysts are not bullish at this price.** Initiating a full position when only Growth and Sentiment are positive (and Sentiment explicitly notes "crowded trade") would be inconsistent with our multi-signal framework.
- **FLAG 3 — Technical regime change in progress.** RSI 36.5, MACD negative, 8/0 sell-signal unanimity, and a -12% slide from the all-time high in 2 weeks. Buying before the consolidation resolves is "catching a falling knife." Prefer waiting for confirmation (either reclaim of NT$2,370 on volume, or a clean test of NT$1,850–1,950 with bullish reversal).
- **FLAG 4 — AI-capex factor concentration.** Verify the portfolio's total AI-capex exposure (NVDA, TSMC, AVGO, VRT, ASML, ARM, ARIA, AMZN, MSFT, META, GOOG, etc.) is below 25–30% **before** adding Delta. If already at the cap, Delta is a *swap candidate*, not an *additive* position.
- **FLAG 5 — Taiwan-domicile cap.** Verify combined Taiwan-listed exposure (TSMC, MediaTek, Hon Hai, Delta) is below 15–20% before sizing.
- **FLAG 6 — Asymmetric downside.** Distance to 50-day MA is ~55%; distance to 200-day MA is ~73%. A real correction would be career-altering at full sizing. Use of a hard stop (e.g., closing position on a weekly close below NT$1,750) or a scaled-in laddered entry is strongly recommended.
- **FLAG 7 — Operating posture.** Treat any initiation as a **starter position (3–4%) with explicit add-on rules**: add 100 bps at NT$1,850 (technical back-test), another 100 bps at NT$1,500–1,700 (fundamentals fair value), and scale toward the 8–10% ceiling only at NT$1,200–1,400 (Buffett strong-buy zone). Do **not** chase strength back to the all-time high without a sentiment/positioning reset.

---

**Risk Manager's bottom line:** Delta is a wonderful business at a demanding price, with a stock chart in volatility-expansion mode. Approve a **3–4% starter position** with a documented add-on ladder. **Do not approve more than 4.5% at current levels.** Revisit sizing if (a) price reaches NT$1,500–1,700, (b) AI-capex guidance from hyperscalers is reaffirmed for 2H 2026, or (c) the stock consolidates for 6–8 weeks and momentum oscillators reset constructively.
