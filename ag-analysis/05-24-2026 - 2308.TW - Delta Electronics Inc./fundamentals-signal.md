## Fundamentals Analyst Signal Report — 2308.TW (Delta Electronics, Inc.)

**Signal:** neutral (business is bullish; valuation is bearish)
**Confidence:** 65

> Originally briefed as "2371.TW" but Delta trades as **2308.TW**. This report is on Delta.

The business is firing on all cylinders — record margins, record revenue, AI-server tailwind. The stock has re-rated so hard that price already embeds aggressive multi-year growth. Fundamentals say "buy"; valuation says "wait." Net: neutral.

### Profitability Metrics
- **Gross margin** (revenue − cost of goods, ÷ revenue; measures pricing power): Q1 2026 hit a record **37.0%** vs 34.6% in Q4 2025 and ~31% a year ago. 2025 full-year ~33.4%. Historical 2020-2023 band was 28-30%. Driver: mix shift to AI-server power supplies, liquid cooling, busbars.
- **Operating margin** (profit before interest/tax ÷ revenue): Q1 2026 record **17.8%**; 2024 ~11.4%; 2025 ~15.8%. Step-change from 11% to ~18% in 12 months is the entire investment story.
- **ROE — Return on Equity** (profit ÷ shareholders' equity): 2025 **~27.2%**; TTM 24.3%. 5-yr historical ~17-20%. Well above Taiwan tech median.
- **ROIC** (profit ÷ debt+equity): estimated TTM ~20-22%, well above ~8-9% cost of capital — every NT$ reinvested creates value.

### Growth Metrics
- **Revenue:** 2023 NT$401B → 2024 NT$421B (+5%) → **2025 NT$554.9B (+31.8%)**. Q1 2026 NT$159.4B (+36% YoY). Q2 2026 guided +20-30% QoQ. 5-yr CAGR ~13-14%.
- **Earnings:** 2025 net income NT$60.1B, EPS NT$23.14. Q1 2026 net income NT$20.6B (+101% YoY), EPS NT$7.91. If Q1 run-rate holds, 2026 EPS could approach NT$35-40 (+50-70% YoY).
- **Free Cash Flow** (operating cash flow − capex): TTM through Dec 2025 ~US$1.66B (~NT$53B); growing ~24%+ YoY.

### Balance Sheet Health
- **Liquidity:** Cash + short-term investments **NT$124-164B** (covers all debt with NT$45-85B left over — **net cash position**).
- **Leverage:** Total debt ~NT$79B vs equity ~NT$267.7B → **D/E ~29.6%**. Conservative for a hardware manufacturer.
- **Interest coverage**: effectively unlimited — Delta earns more from its cash pile than it pays in interest. Zero distress risk.

### Cash Flow Quality
- **OCF vs Net Income:** OCF historically meets or exceeds net income; debt ~95% covered by a single year of OCF. Clean accruals.
- **FCF Conversion** (FCF ÷ Net Income): 2025e ~88% — very healthy.
- **Capex:** Guided modestly higher in 2025-26 for AI-power capacity (Thailand, India, Mexico/US). Doesn't dent FCF.

### Earnings Quality Notes
**Positives:** ~88% FCF conversion; net cash balance sheet rules out leverage-driven EPS games; margin expansion is mix-driven (real), not one-time gains.

**Yellow flags:**
- **Margin sustainability:** 37% GM is unprecedented; could normalize as Lite-On/Acbel/Vertiv ramp capacity. Model 32-34% steady-state.
- **Customer concentration:** Heavy hyperscaler / NVIDIA-ecosystem exposure, not disclosed in detail.
- **Working capital:** +36% revenue growth will inflate receivables/inventory — watch DSO and inventory days in upcoming filings.
- **FX:** ~80% of revenue is non-TWD; TWD strength compresses reported margins.
- **Segment disclosure:** Management hasn't cleanly broken out AI-server revenue.

No accounting red flags spotted. Taiwan IFRS reporting is generally high quality.

### Bottom Line
Business: bullish 80/100. Stock: bearish 40/100 — multiple already prices in flawless multi-year execution. Combined: **neutral, confidence 65**. Pullback to NT$1,500-1,700 (fwd P/E ~35-40x) would make this a strong buy. At NT$2,095, risk/reward is unattractive.
