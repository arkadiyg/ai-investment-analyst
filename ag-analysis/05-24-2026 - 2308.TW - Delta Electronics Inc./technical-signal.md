## Technical Analyst Signal Report — 2308.TW

**Signal:** neutral (with a near-term bearish tilt)
**Confidence:** 60
**Verified Current Price:** NT$2,095.00 (source: https://www.investing.com/equities/delta-electron-technical, date: 2026-05-23)

*Context — for the new investor:* Delta Electronics (a Taiwan-listed maker of power management gear, EV components, and AI server cooling/power supplies) has had a roughly **6x rally over the last 12 months**, climbing from a 52-week low of ~NT$365 to an all-time high of NT$2,370 on May 7, 2026. The stock has since pulled back about 12% to NT$2,095. The technical picture has to be read in that "parabolic move now cooling off" context.

---

### Trend Assessment

**Long-term trend: Strongly bullish.** The 200-day moving average (a "moving average" is the average closing price over the last N trading days — it smooths daily noise into a trend line) sits near **NT$559.85**. Price at NT$2,095 is trading **~+275% above the 200-day** — an extreme reading that only appears in stocks experiencing a once-in-a-decade re-rating (in Delta's case, the AI/data-center power story).

**Medium-term trend: Still bullish but stretched.** The 50-day moving average sits near **NT$923.66** — price is roughly **+127% above the 50-day**. Healthy uptrending stocks usually trade 5–20% above their 50-day MA; >100% above is parabolic territory and historically resolves with either a multi-month sideways base or a sharper retracement.

**Short-term trend: Cooling.** The 12% pullback from the NT$2,370 peak (May 7) to NT$2,095 (May 23) is the first real correction in months. The 50-day MA is rising steeply but is still **NT$1,170 below price** — meaning the stock could fall a long way before that long-term support line is even tested.

*Bottom line on trend:* Long-term uptrend fully intact; short-term momentum has rolled over.

### Momentum State

Momentum indicators measure **how fast** price is moving, not just direction. All three are flashing caution:

- **RSI (Relative Strength Index, 14-day): 36.5.** RSI runs 0–100; readings above 70 are "overbought" (stretched up), below 30 are "oversold" (stretched down). At **36.5**, RSI has fallen sharply from likely overbought levels at the May 7 peak and is now approaching — but not yet in — oversold territory. This signals that **upward momentum has clearly broken**, even though price is still high. It is *not* yet a contrarian buy signal.

- **MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence): -0.17, negative.** MACD compares two moving averages; a negative reading means the shorter-term average has crossed below the longer-term one — a classic short-term **sell signal**. The reading is only mildly negative, suggesting the bearish momentum shift is early-stage, not exhausted.

- **Stochastic Oscillator:** Investing.com's aggregate technical readout shows **8 sell signals and 0 buy signals across the standard momentum oscillator set** (RSI, MACD, Stochastic, CCI, ADX, Williams %R, etc.) — a "Strong Sell" daily technical rating. This is a near-unanimous short-term bearish vote from momentum tools.

*Caveat for the new investor:* "Strong Sell" momentum readings on a stock 275% above its 200-day MA usually mean **"the rally is taking a breather,"** not **"the trend is over."** Momentum tools flip negative routinely during normal pullbacks in strong uptrends.

Detailed daily volume series for 2308.TW was not directly retrievable through our MCP feed (Taiwan coverage gap), so this section relies on observable price-action context:

- The May 7 all-time high at NT$2,370 followed by an immediate ~12% slide is the classic shape of a **"blow-off" or climax pattern** — heavy buying enthusiasm followed by distribution (insiders/large holders selling into retail demand).
- The trading-range compression of the last two weeks (NT$2,050–NT$2,125 on May 23 alone is a NT$75, ~3.6% intraday range — still wide) suggests **volatility remains elevated** rather than a calm post-rally rest.
- Whether this is **accumulation** (smart money quietly buying the dip, which would set up another leg higher) or **distribution** (smart money selling into bounces, which would set up a deeper correction) cannot be confirmed without on-balance-volume data for this ticker. The momentum-indicator unanimity (8/0 sell) leans toward **distribution** as the more likely interpretation for the next 2–6 weeks.
