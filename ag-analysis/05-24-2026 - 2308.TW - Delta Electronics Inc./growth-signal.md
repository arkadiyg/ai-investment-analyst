### Plain-English primer (for first-time readers)

- **Delta Electronics** designs and builds the boxes that convert electricity from one form to another — wall power into the low-voltage DC current that chips need. Inside every AI server rack are stacks of these "power shelves," and Delta is the dominant supplier.
- **PSU** = Power Supply Unit, the brick that feeds a server.
- **HVDC (High-Voltage DC)** = a newer way to deliver power inside a data center using ~400V or ~800V direct current instead of normal alternating current. It loses less energy as heat. NVIDIA is pushing the industry to 800V HVDC for its next-gen "AI factories" because AI racks now draw up to 1 megawatt each.
- **GB200 / GB300** = NVIDIA's current and next-generation AI server "racks." Each rack needs up to eight separate power supply racks to feed it. Delta makes those.
- **CDU (Coolant Distribution Unit)** = the plumbing manifold for liquid cooling, which has replaced air cooling for high-density AI racks.
- **SOFC (Solid Oxide Fuel Cell)** = an on-site power-generation device that turns natural gas or hydrogen directly into electricity at ~65% efficiency.

### TAM Analysis

Delta sits at the intersection of three enormous, compounding markets:

1. **AI server power supplies (the core engine right now).** Independent forecasts size the AI data-center HVDC power-supply market in the multi-tens of billions USD by the early 2030s, growing 25-35% per year. Delta + Vertiv together already control >40% of this niche, and the top five players hold ~65%. Delta is #1 in unit share with ~15% of all server PSUs globally and >6M PSUs/year of capacity. With AI rack density forecast to surge from ~50 kW today to ~1 MW by 2029, every NVIDIA rack generation requires more dollars of Delta content per rack.
2. **Data-center power & cooling infrastructure (the next leg).** Delta is moving up the stack from selling PSUs (component) to selling integrated 800V HVDC In-Row Power Racks (sub-system) plus liquid-cooling CDUs plus battery backup (BBU) plus microgrid and on-site SOFC generation. Dollar content per data-center MW expands roughly 3-5x. This is a >$50B annual TAM by late decade.
3. **EV powertrain + industrial automation + energy infrastructure.** Delta's "Mobility" segment supplies EV inverters, on-board chargers, and DC fast-charging. Combined with industrial automation and renewables, this is another $30-50B addressable pool where Delta is top-3 globally.

**Penetration math:** Delta's run-rate revenue is ~NT$640B (~US$20B). Combined accessible TAM is conservatively US$150-200B+ by 2030. Delta could plausibly grow into US$40-50B by 2030 — a 2-2.5x topline expansion in five years.

### Competitive Positioning

Power supplies aren't a "network-effect" business in the social-network sense, but they exhibit **winner-take-most dynamics** through three reinforcing flywheels:

1. **Design-in lock-in with NVIDIA.** Delta co-developed the 800 VDC In-Row 660kW Power Rack architecture with NVIDIA, demonstrated jointly at NVIDIA GTC in March 2026. Once a power architecture is co-engineered with NVIDIA and qualified at hyperscaler customers, switching cost is enormous.
2. **Vertical integration up the stack.** Delta ships **PSU + power shelf + in-row power rack + CDU + BBU + microgrid + SOFC generation** under one roof. Competitors typically own one or two of those.
3. **Scale and capital advantage.** Delta produces >6M PSUs/year. Unit-cost curve and supply-chain leverage with magnetics, capacitors, and SiC/GaN semis is structural.

Delta is the **only** player credibly attacking the full stack from medium-voltage transformer to chip-level VRM, plus on-site generation. That breadth is the durable moat.

### Key Catalysts

1. **GB300 NVL72 ramp through 2H 2026 and into 2027.** Each rack demands 8 power racks; Delta supplies the reference design. Hyperscaler capex from the four major US CSPs is already +60% YoY in Q1 2026.
2. **HVDC ±400V small-volume shipments starting 2H 2026, full 800V deployment in 2027.** Each transition is an ASP step-up per rack.
3. **Liquid-cooling CDU attach rate.** Delta sells power and cooling together, doubling content per rack.
4. **SOFC mass-production launch in 2027** (sampling in 2026). With US grid interconnects taking 4-7 years, SOFC could open a brand-new multi-billion-dollar adjacency.
5. **EV powertrain wins.** Underappreciated growth leg per Morningstar.
6. **Microgrid / Solid State Transformer (SST) sales** to AI factories.

### Risks to Growth Thesis

1. **Valuation is the biggest near-term risk.** P/E ~42x trailing on the conservative measure; one source pegs it at ~90x. A hyperscaler capex pause or a Lite-On share-win headline could compress the multiple sharply.
2. **AI capex digestion.** The +60% YoY CSP capex print is unsustainable forever.
3. **Share loss to Lite-On.**
4. **NVIDIA concentration / architecture shift.**
5. **Geopolitics / Taiwan risk.**
6. **800V execution.** Solid-state transformers, BBU integration, and safety certification are genuinely hard. A 6-12 month slip lets Vertiv close the gap.
7. **EV powertrain margin pressure** from China price war.
8. **SOFC commercialization risk.** Field reliability, fuel logistics, and cost per kW are unproven at scale.

### Bottom line

Delta is one of the rare "shovels" plays where the company is both (a) a near-monopoly supplier to the fastest-growing capex category of this decade, and (b) has multiple optional second acts. Q1 2026 confirms a real inflection. The reservation is price — the stock has already absorbed a lot of the story. Sizing should reflect that — a starter position with room to add on any AI-capex-fear pullback is the prudent way to express the thesis.
